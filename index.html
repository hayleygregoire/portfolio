About Me

I'm Hayley Gregoire, a graduate student at Northwestern University's Medill School of Journalism specializing in sports media. My goal is to become an on-air sideline reporter, bringing compelling stories from the field to sports fans everywhere.

Currently, I work as a Basketball Communications Associate with the Chicago Bulls, where I transcribe press conferences and manage postgame audio distribution. This role has deepened my understanding of professional sports media operations and strengthened my ability to work efficiently under tight deadlines.

I'm bilingual in English and Spanish, which has proven invaluable in connecting with diverse communities and sources. My adaptability has been one of my greatest strengths—I've thrived in various settings, from teaching English to elementary students in Spain, to supporting production for the Tony Awards in New York, to working as a video production intern for SiriusXM. Whether I'm creating digital content, conducting interviews, or working behind the scenes at live events, I bring enthusiasm, attention to detail, and a commitment to excellence.

I graduated summa cum laude from Fordham University with a B.A. in Communications & Culture and Spanish, and was also a student in the Honors College.

Beat Interests: Football, tennis, athlete advocacy, diversity in sports media

This page was built by me. I used generative AI (Claude) for initial HTML/CSS template creation, layout brainstorming, placeholder text generation, and CSS refactoring. All content, biographical information, and final design decisions were reviewed, edited, and customized by me.

As for the website design choices, I knew the colors I wanted in my portfolio (such as sage, light cream and terracotta), so I found the hex codes and entered them into Claude. These colors are warm and inviting, but also elegant. I used a photo that I had and got a url for it, and entered it into Claude to include it in the HTML code. Lastly, I wanted my website to look elegant but easy to navigate. Nothing is worse than a website with too many links and it's not user-friendly, so accessibility was something that I prioritized. I wanted everything on the same page with categories at the top that allows the user to skip to a certain section, so I included this in my prompt to Claude.
